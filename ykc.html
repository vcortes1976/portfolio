YOGA KIND COLLECTIVE

UI | Mobile App | iOS Design | Material Design

DISCOVER

Yoga Kind Collective is a non-profit group of yoga teachers offering a variety of yoga classes. Our key values are:

Inclusive - We understand that yoga isn't for everybody but we do believe it is for anybody; we strive to make our offerings accessible to anyone.

Open - We are an open and diverse community of yoga teachers and lifelong students, dedicated to connecting and sharing our passion and knowledge with you.

Whole - Yoga isn't just a workout, it's a work in. It is the union of mind, body, and spirit.

As co-founder of the Collective, I am developing a brand and community for our teachers and students.

My role:
Competitor analysis
Gathering requirements
Usability testing and analysis
iOS and Material app design
Logo design and style guide

Project duration:
Approximately 4 weeks.

DEFINE

Key objectives:

  • Design a mobile app for iOS and Android devices.
  • Brand awareness.
  • Schedule and promote classes.
  • Give users the freedom to book/cancel classes easily wherever they are.
  • Reward users with relevant content i.e. guided meditations, podcasts, videos, daily inspirations, blog posts etc.
  • Build a community.

DEVELOP

User flow diagram - Once the objectives and key requirements of the app were determined, I was able to create user stories and produce specific user flows.

Wireflow - I created mid-fidelity wireframes in iOS first, following design guidelines, developing linear icons and showing the interactions using gestures. I followed the same process when designing the Android version.

Competitor research and inspiration - I researched competitor apps for design patterns including sound and animation. I found many yoga apps on the market including Alo Moves, Vagaro, Glo, DownDog, Yoga For Beginners, and the more well known brands had a fresh, minimal feel i.e. white background with colour accents and images to support the design, with simple buttons and key functions. Our logo had a similar look and feel which I wanted to carry across into the app design, so I opted to use a colour accent from the logo on a tinted white background, with grey and black as secondary colours. I also used images and placed colour overlays to tie the design together.
